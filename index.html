Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to the most common questions about buying, selling, and investing in real estate

Buying a home is a significant financial decision. You're likely ready when you have a stable income, good credit score (typically 620+), enough savings for a down payment (usually 3-20%), and an emergency fund. Our financial planning experts can help assess your readiness and guide you through pre-approval.

Spring (March-May) is traditionally the best time to sell, with more buyers actively searching. However, the "right time" depends on your local market conditions, personal circumstances, and property type. Our market analysis can identify the optimal timing for your specific situation.

Your offer should be based on comparable sales (comps), market conditions, property condition, and your budget. In a seller's market, you may need to offer at or above asking price. In a buyer's market, there's more room for negotiation. Our agents provide detailed comparative market analyses to guide your offer strategy.

Closing costs typically range from 2-5% of the purchase price and include loan origination fees, appraisal fees, title insurance, attorney fees, and prepaid items like property taxes and homeowners insurance. Both buyers and sellers have closing costs, though buyers generally pay more. We provide detailed estimates so there are no surprises.

Absolutely. A professional home inspection (typically $300-$500) can reveal hidden issues with the property's structure, systems, and safety. This information is crucial for negotiating repairs or price adjustments. We can recommend trusted inspectors and help you understand the findings.

On average, buying takes 30-60 days from accepted offer to closing, while selling takes 65-93 days from listing to closing. However, these timelines vary based on market conditions, financing type, and property specifics. Cash buyers can close in as little as 1-2 weeks.
